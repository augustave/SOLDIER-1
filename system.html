Doctrines

Two surfaces, one system

The system splits on material logic. Doctrine A is smooth molded plastic — neumorphic hardware. Doctrine B is paper and ink — archival manila. They share tokens (color, spacing, monospace) and split on display type.

Doctrine A
Civic Plastic
Smooth ABS/PC surface · neumorphic shadows · stenciled industrial display · pixel-CRT mono. Reads as molded equipment.
Display: Stencil Surface: #EAEAEA
Doctrine B
Archival Record
Manila paper · blue ink · photocopied newsprint serif · pixel-CRT mono. Reads as declassified document.
Display: Redaction Surface: #F4EFE6

Typography

Five families. Use stencil for plastic display, serif (Redaction) for paper display, display (Space Grotesk) for body and mid-size headings, mono (VT323) for labels and telemetry, editorial (Instrument Serif italic) for one pull-quote moment per page.
